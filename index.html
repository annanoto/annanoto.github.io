About

Who I am

College, Creativity, and Makerspaces in between.

I'm a Computer Engineering graduate from the University of the Pacific, heading to Northeastern University this fall to pursue an M.S. in Advanced and Intelligent Manufacturing. My work spans from embedded firmware and network security on the engineering side, to laser cutting, embroidery, woodworking, and 3D printing on the maker side — with photography and graphic design running through all of it.

I've spent the past few years working at the Fletcher Jones Foundation Makerspace at UOP — operating the equipment, training students, maintaining certifications, and building my own projects whenever I get a chance. I care about finishing things, documenting them well, and making sure the next person can pick up where I left off or repeat the project on their own.

I'm a Girl Scout Gold Award recipient, an Alumnus Member of Theta Tau, and a volunteer counselor at Camp Oasis for the Crohn's & Colitis Foundation. Whether I'm building something from scratch, working through an engineering problem, or producing media through my own small business, I bring a structured but creative approach to everything I do.
