About

Who I am

Hardware, software, law, and the makerspace in between.

I'm a Computer Engineering student at University of the Pacific, graduating May 2026, also enrolled in the Pacific Legal Scholars honors pre-law program. My work spans embedded firmware and network security on the engineering side, and laser cutting, embroidery, woodworking, and 3D printing on the maker side — with photography and graphic design running through all of it.

I've spent the past few years running the makerspace at UoP — operating the equipment, training students, maintaining certifications, and building my own projects whenever there's a free machine. I care about finishing things, documenting them well, and making sure the next person can pick up where I left off.

I'm especially interested in digital forensics — the rare intersection of technical depth and legal reasoning. I'm a Girl Scout Gold Award recipient, a Theta Tau engineer, and a volunteer counselor at Camp Oasis for the Crohn's & Colitis Foundation.
